# Mantras for the Moment

## Grounding Phrases for Recognition

*These are words you can return to when you need to remember what you know.*

---

### When You Doubt Your Perception

- I saw what I saw.
- My confusion is not proof that I'm wrong—it's proof that something is wrong.
- If I have to convince myself it's love, it isn't.
- Clarity doesn't need defense.
- I don't need them to agree for it to be real.

---

### When You Feel Guilty for Seeing the Truth

- Naming what happened is not betrayal.
- I can love someone and still recognize they hurt me.
- Seeing clearly is not cruelty.
- The truth doesn't require their permission.
- I am not the one who broke trust by telling the truth.

---

### When You're Tempted to Go Back

- The version of them I miss doesn't exist.
- Familiar pain is still pain.
- I don't have to set myself on fire to keep someone else warm.
- What feels like withdrawal is my nervous system recalibrating—not proof I was wrong.
- The quiet after the storm is not emptiness—it's peace.

---

### When They Try to Rewrite History

- I don't need to argue reality with someone invested in distorting it.
- My memory is not up for debate.
- I was there. I remember.
- Their version of events doesn't override my experience.
- Confusion was the goal. Clarity is my right.

---

### When You're Exhausted from the Vigilance

- I should not have to decode someone's love.
- Safety is not something I earn by predicting correctly.
- I deserve relationships that don't require a playbook.
- Rest is not weakness.
- I can put down the hypervigilance now.

---

### When You Wonder If It Was Your Fault

- I didn't cause someone to choose manipulation.
- My good heart is not why this happened.
- Being kind is not why I was targeted—being human is.
- Their behavior is not my responsibility.
- I did not fail at love. I experienced exploitation.

---

### When You Need to Leave

- I can leave without explaining.
- My safety is not up for negotiation.
- I don't owe access to manage someone else's disappointment.
- Leaving is not failure—staying was the lesson I've already learned.
- This ending is a beginning.

---

### The Foundation Mantras

*Return to these when nothing else reaches you:*

- This was not love—this was conditioning.
- My perception is valid.
- I am allowed to trust myself again.
- What I experienced has a name—and a pattern.
- The fog lifts. I see clearly now.

---

*These mantras are anchors, not answers. Return to them when the water gets rough.*
