**After (Current):**

>

## Part 3: Additional Claims Verification (Round 2)

### 3.1: Extinction Burst ✅ VERIFIED

**Location:** `chapters/11-romantic-manipulation.md`, line 420

**Claim:** "When a narcissist senses they're losing control...they often escalate dramatically. This is sometimes called the 'extinction burst': a final, desperate attempt to reassert dominance."

**Verification:** The extinction burst is a well-established behavioral psychology concept. Definition from academic sources: "The extinction burst is an increase in an operant behavior early in the transition to extinction...a sudden and temporary increase in the response's frequency, followed by the eventual decline and extinction of the behavior."

**Assessment:** The manuscript's application of this behavioral concept to relationship dynamics is appropriate and well-supported.

**Sources:**
- ScienceDirect: Extinction Burst overview
- PMC (2023): A Theory of the Extinction Burst
- PMC (2023): Basic and applied research on extinction bursts

**Location:** `chapters/01-opening-manifesto.md`, line 119

**Claim:** "Your prefrontal cortex—the part of your brain responsible for judgment and decision-making—comes back online."

**Assessment:** Accurate characterization. Fully supported by neuroscience research.

### 3.3: Extended Exhale and Parasympathetic Activation ✅ VERIFIED

**Location:** `chapters/17-energetic-remapping.md`, line 78

**Claim:** "Breathe in for 4 counts, out for 8 counts. The extended exhale activates the parasympathetic nervous system—the 'rest and digest' mode that calms activation."

**Verification:** Strong scientific support:
- "During prolonged expiratory breathing, parasympathetic nervous function was significantly activated." (PMC 2018)
- "Longer exhalations phasically and tonically stimulate the vagus nerve." (Leiden University review)
- A 2023 Stanford study showed cyclic sighing (prolonged exhalation) "led to the greatest daily increases in positive emotions and decreased breaths per minute."

**Assessment:** Accurate. The specific 4:8 ratio aligns with recommended practices and the mechanism described is scientifically validated.

**Sources:**
- PMC: The relaxation effect of prolonged expiratory breathing
- Psychology Today: Longer Exhalations Are an Easy Way to Hack Your Vagus Nerve
