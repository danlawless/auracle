## What Is the Field?

Your field is the energetic extension of your nervous system state.

It's not mystical first—it's physiological first.

When you're regulated, your heart creates a coherent electromagnetic field that extends several feet beyond your body. Others can feel it. Research at the HeartMath Institute has measured it.

When you're dysregulated, that field becomes chaotic. Others feel that too—though they may not know what they're feeling.

**Your field is what people sense when they say:**
- "I feel calm around you"
- "Something about them makes me uneasy"
- "The energy in that room was heavy"
- "They have presence"

This isn't mystical language. It's accurate perception of a real phenomenon.

## Neuroscience Lens: The Physiological Field

Your nervous system communicates beyond words.

**Heart Coherence:**
The heart generates the body's largest electromagnetic field—60 times stronger than the brain's. When you're in a coherent state (regulated, calm, present), this field becomes ordered and measurable at a distance.

**Vagal Tone:**
Your vagus nerve connects your brain to your body. High vagal tone correlates with emotional flexibility, social attunement, and what we're calling "field coherence."

**Mirror Neurons:**
Your brain contains neurons that fire both when you perform an action and when you observe someone else performing it. This creates automatic attunement—you feel what others feel, often without knowing.

**Entrainment:**
When two rhythmic systems come into proximity, they tend to synchronize. Humans entrain to each other's nervous system states. The most stable nervous system often leads the entrainment.

**Key insight:** Your field isn't separate from your body. It's your body, extended. What you regulate inside radiates outside.

**Translation:** Working on your field IS working on your nervous system. They're the same thing.

## Sensitivity vs. Boundarylessness

Many sensitive people believe their sensitivity is the problem.

It's not.

**Sensitivity is the capacity to perceive. Boundarylessness is the failure to differentiate.**

You can be highly sensitive AND have a coherent field. In fact, sensitivity becomes a gift when it's paired with good energetic hygiene.

The goal isn't to feel less. It's to know whose feeling you're feeling.

## Where Do You Leak Attention?

Before you can train your field, you need to know where it's already going.

**Common attention leaks:**
- Scanning others' moods before attending to your own
- Monitoring the room for threats or needs
- Running mental simulations of how others might react
- Broadcasting "I'm safe, I'm not a threat" signals
- Performing competence, calm, or control

These leaks developed for good reason. They kept you safe. But they also drain the field.

**The goal isn't to stop caring about others.** It's to stop outsourcing your nervous system to them.

## A Practice: The 60-Second Field Scan

Do this once a day, or before entering charged spaces.

**Step 1:** Stand or sit comfortably. Close your eyes.

**Step 2:** Imagine your field as a bubble around your body—about arm's length in all directions.

**Step 3:** Scan each direction:
- **Front:** What are you projecting forward? (Performance? Protection? Openness?)
- **Back:** What are you guarding against behind you? What are you carrying?
- **Left/Right:** Where are you reaching toward others? Where are you bracing?
- **Above/Below:** How grounded are you? How much space do you allow yourself?

**Step 4:** Without changing anything, just notice. Where is your field clear? Where is it cluttered, defended, or collapsed?

**Step 5:** Take three breaths. Return to the room.

---

## A Practice: Return to Sender

Use this when you've absorbed energy that isn't yours.

**Step 1:** Notice you're carrying something that doesn't belong to you. (Mood, tension, anxiety, heaviness)

**Step 2:** Place a hand on your chest. Say internally or aloud:
*"This is not mine. I release it with compassion. Return to sender."*

**Step 3:** Imagine the energy lifting from your field—not with hostility, but with clarity. You're not attacking. You're differentiating.

**Step 4:** Fill the space with your own breath, your own presence.

**Note:** This isn't about blaming others for your state. It's about cleaning your field without making it mean something.
