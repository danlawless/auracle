**Context:** For survivors with complex trauma, dissociation, or chronic pain, body signals may be numbed, sending false alarms (PTSD), conflicting, or overwhelming to access.

## Category 2: Mental Health Terminology & Stigma (Q4-Q6)

### Q4: "Narcissist" Terminology and Stigma

**Current State:** Uses "narcissist" throughout while noting it's not diagnosing anyone.

**Context:** Some clinicians worry that popularizing "narcissist" as shorthand for "abusive person" stigmatizes people with NPD (a treatable condition) while missing that many abusers don't have NPD.

**The Question:** What's your intent around people with diagnosed NPD who are in treatment? The line between describing patterns vs. creating a villain archetype?

**Options:**
- [ ] A) Add explicit note distinguishing behavior patterns from clinical diagnosis
- [ ] B) Add acknowledgment that some with NPD do seek treatment and change
- [ ] C) Keep current framing — this book serves survivors, not those with NPD
- [ ] D) Soften villain archetype language where possible
- [ ] E) Other approach (please specify)

---

### Q5: "Lack of Empathy" and Neurodivergence

**Current State:** Describes narcissists as having "impaired empathy" or "lack of empathy."

**Context:** Autistic advocates have raised concerns about "lack of empathy" language conflating autism with harmful intent. Autistic empathy is present but processed differently.

**The Question:** How might you clarify you're describing willful disregard, not neurodivergent processing differences?

**Options:**
- [ ] A) Reframe as "lack of empathic care" or "empathy withheld"
- [ ] B) Add note distinguishing narcissistic lack of empathy from autism
- [ ] C) Use "instrumental empathy" (can read but doesn't care) vs "different empathy expression"
- [ ] D) Current context makes distinction clear
- [ ] E) Other approach (please specify)
