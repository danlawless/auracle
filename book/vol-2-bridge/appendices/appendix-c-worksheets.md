## 2. Family Role Identification

Check all that apply for each role:

### Hero / Parentified Child
- ☐ I took responsibility early in my family
- ☐ I was praised for being "mature" or "wise"
- ☐ I often feel responsible for others' emotions
- ☐ I feel needed more than cherished in relationships
- ☐ I struggle to let others take care of me

### Scapegoat
- ☐ I was often blamed for family problems
- ☐ I was told I was "too sensitive" or "dramatic"
- ☐ I tend to over-explain myself
- ☐ I doubt my own perception frequently
- ☐ I stay in relationships to prove my worth

### Lost Child
- ☐ I learned early that my needs didn't matter
- ☐ I tend to be invisible or overlooked
- ☐ I accept less than I deserve in relationships
- ☐ I rarely ask for what I need
- ☐ I romanticize longing and distance

### Mascot / Entertainer
- ☐ I use humor to diffuse tension
- ☐ I feel responsible for lightening the mood
- ☐ My real emotions rarely get expressed
- ☐ I attract partners who don't go deep
- ☐ I feel unseen emotionally

### Golden Child
- ☐ I was put on a pedestal
- ☐ I fear falling from grace
- ☐ I struggle when I'm not exceptional
- ☐ I have shame around imperfection
- ☐ I attract admiration more than intimacy

### Peacekeeper
- ☐ I avoid conflict at almost any cost
- ☐ I suppress my needs to maintain harmony
- ☐ I explode or leave suddenly after long periods of silence
- ☐ I have difficulty expressing disagreement
- ☐ I feel unsafe when there's tension

## 3. Attachment Style Quick Check

Answer honestly for your current patterns (not how you'd like to be):

### When I'm stressed in a relationship, I tend to:
- ☐ A: Reach out more, seek reassurance, worry about abandonment
- ☐ B: Pull away, need space, feel overwhelmed by closeness
- ☐ C: Oscillate between reaching and pulling away
- ☐ D: Stay calm, communicate, neither cling nor flee

### When my partner is upset with me, I:
- ☐ A: Feel anxious, try to fix it immediately, need to know we're okay
- ☐ B: Feel trapped, want space, may shut down
- ☐ C: Feel confused, react differently each time, feel chaotic
- ☐ D: Listen, take responsibility where appropriate, stay present

### In terms of closeness, I:
- ☐ A: Often want more closeness than my partner
- ☐ B: Often need more space than my partner
- ☐ C: Want closeness then suddenly need space
- ☐ D: Feel comfortable with both closeness and space

**Mostly A's:** Anxious-Preoccupied
**Mostly B's:** Dismissive-Avoidant
**Mostly C's:** Fearful-Avoidant/Disorganized
**Mostly D's:** Secure

## 4. Emotional Immaturity vs. Secure Capacity Decoder

Use this to assess dynamics in your relationships:

| Emotional Immaturity | Secure Emotional Capacity |
|---------------------|--------------------------|
| Escalates when challenged | Pauses and regulates |
| Needs agreement to feel okay | Tolerates difference |
| Makes others responsible for feelings | Owns their internal state |
| Cannot tolerate discomfort | Can sit with tension |
| Moralizes or intellectualizes feelings | Feels without judging |
| Repair requires control or collapse | Repairs without drama |
| Criticism feels like attack | Feedback is information |

## 6. Dating Phase Checklist: The First 90 Days

### Weeks 1-4: Observation

**Green Flags Present:**
- ☐ Consistent follow-through
- ☐ Respectful pacing
- ☐ Genuine curiosity
- ☐ Can hear "no" gracefully
- ☐ Stable mood across interactions

**Red Flags Absent:**
- ☐ No love-bombing
- ☐ No pressure to escalate
- ☐ No victimhood about all exes
- ☐ No dismissing of your concerns
- ☐ Words and actions align

**My Internal State:**
- ☐ I feel regulated after dates
- ☐ I'm not obsessing between dates
- ☐ I'm observing, not projecting

---

### Weeks 5-8: Calibration

**Growth Signals:**
- ☐ Can tolerate feedback without defensiveness
- ☐ Maintains interest after a "no"
- ☐ Follows through consistently
- ☐ Shows curiosity about my inner world
- ☐ Respects my pacing

**Concerns:**
- ☐ Retaliation or withdrawal after boundaries
- ☐ Needs to be right
- ☐ Subtle criticism or correction
- ☐ Avoidance of emotional topics
- ☐ Inconsistent effort

---

### Weeks 9-12: Integration

**Positive Indicators:**
- ☐ Consistent presence (not just early effort)
- ☐ Interest in my life outside the relationship
- ☐ Can disagree without drama
- ☐ Integration feels natural
- ☐ I'm becoming more myself

**Warning Signs:**
- ☐ Pressure for quick exclusivity
- ☐ Isolation from friends/family
- ☐ Escalating intensity without proportional time
- ☐ I'm adapting to them more than they're meeting me
- ☐ Relationship already feels like work

## 8. Integration Plan: 6-Month Overview

### Month 1-2: Stabilize
**Focus:** Establish regulated baseline
**Goals:**
- ☐ Complete Weeks 1-4 of Secure Attachment Training
- ☐ Develop 3 reliable regulation practices
- ☐ Begin tracking nervous system states

### Month 3: Contain
**Focus:** Increase distress tolerance
**Goals:**
- ☐ Complete Weeks 5-6 of Training
- ☐ Practice sitting with discomfort
- ☐ Reduce reassurance-seeking

### Month 4: Differentiate
**Focus:** Develop self as separate
**Goals:**
- ☐ Complete Weeks 7-9 of Training
- ☐ Practice expressing preferences
- ☐ Hold own reality without validation

### Month 5: Integrate
**Focus:** Apply in relationships
**Goals:**
- ☐ Complete Weeks 10-12 of Training
- ☐ Express needs directly
- ☐ Practice repair

### Month 6: Consolidate
**Focus:** Make practices second nature
**Goals:**
- ☐ Review and refine practices
- ☐ Assess relationship changes
- ☐ Set intentions for continued growth
