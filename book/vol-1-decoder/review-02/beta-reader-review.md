## Final Assessment

### The Book Delivers On Its Promise

This manuscript does what it sets out to do:
- Validates survivor experience without pathologizing
- Explains psychological mechanisms in accessible language
- Provides practical, immediately usable tools
- Centers body awareness and somatic experience
- Offers hope without toxic positivity

### Would I Recommend This Book?

**To someone just escaping:** Yes, with guidance. Start with Front Matter → Chapter 16 → Chapter 14. Read Chapters 1-4 when stabilized. Wait on Chapter 17.

**To someone in stabilization (3-6 months out):** Absolutely. Read cover to cover with breaks as needed. This book will become a reference you return to.

**To someone helping a survivor:** Yes. The Decoder Cards and Chapter 13 questions are invaluable for supporting without rescuing.

### The Ending Satisfies

Chapter 19 lands the emotional arc:
- Acknowledges complexity of leaving
- Gives permission for messy feelings
- Offers practical next steps
- Closes with hope grounded in reality

### Bailey's Signature Insight

The "what it feels like in your body" sections throughout the manuscript are what elevate this from a good book to an essential one. Survivors don't just need to understand narcissism intellectually — they need to recognize it in their nervous system. This book does that consistently and compassionately.

The remaining issues (Chapter 6 ending, Chapter 18 density) are fixable within 2-3 editing passes. The foundation is strong. The heart is there. The expertise is evident.
