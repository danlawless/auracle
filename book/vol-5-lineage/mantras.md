# Mantras for the Moment

## Grounding Phrases for Parenting After Trauma

*These are words you can return to when the weight of what you're trying to break feels unbearable.*

---

### When You've Made a Mistake

- Repair is more powerful than perfection.
- I can apologize without shame spiraling.
- One moment does not define my parenting.
- They don't need me flawless. They need me present.
- I can start again in the next breath.

---

### When the Rage Rises

- This feeling is old. My child is not the source.
- I can pause before I respond.
- Noticing the activation is already breaking the chain.
- I am allowed to feel rage. I don't have to act from it.
- My nervous system is triggered. That's information, not instruction.

---

### When Your Child Mirrors Your Wound

- This child is not here to teach me a lesson.
- What they reflect is not their fault.
- I can see my pain in them and still parent them clearly.
- My wound is mine to heal—not theirs to fix.
- I can love them fully even when they activate me.

---

### When You're Co-Parenting With Difficulty

- I cannot control what happens in their home.
- I can only offer what I can offer.
- My consistency is powerful even when it feels small.
- I protect them by regulating myself.
- I am not responsible for my ex's behavior—only my own.

---

### When You're Terrified of Passing It On

- Awareness is already the first break in the chain.
- I don't have to be healed to be a good parent. I have to be healing.
- My children don't need a perfect parent. They need a conscious one.
- What I'm giving them by trying is already more than I received.
- The chain breaks with me—not because I'm perfect, but because I'm aware.

---

### When Shame Follows an Imperfect Moment

- I am not the worst version of my parenting.
- Shame tells me I care. It doesn't tell me who I am.
- I can grieve what I did without becoming it.
- Every rupture is a chance to model repair.
- My children will remember how I recovered, not just how I fell.

---

### When Your Nervous System Is Maxed

- I can take a pause. My child will survive my pause.
- I am allowed to regulate before I respond.
- My needs matter, even in parenting.
- I cannot pour from empty. Rest is not abandonment.
- I can love them and need space at the same time.

---

### When You Compare Yourself to Other Parents

- I don't know what happens behind their doors.
- My healing journey makes my parenting different—not worse.
- I'm parenting while also reparenting myself. That's extraordinary.
- Comparison steals presence. I choose to stay here.
- My children don't need a different parent. They need me, healing.

---

### The Foundation Mantras

*Return to these when nothing else reaches you:*

- The chain breaks with me. Not because I'm perfect, but because I'm aware.
- Repair over perfection. Always.
- My children don't need me healed. They need me healing.
- I am already enough to begin.
- What I'm giving them is more than I received—and that's everything.

---

*These mantras are not absolution. They're steadying. Return to them when you forget you're doing something sacred.*
