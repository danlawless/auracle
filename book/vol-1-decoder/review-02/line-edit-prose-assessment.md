## Voice Evaluation: Round 2

### Voice Strengths (Maintained)

1. **Somatic Grounding**: The "What it feels like in your body" sections remain exceptional and consistent throughout.

2. **Second-Person Direct Address**: Maintained with high consistency. The manuscript speaks directly to the reader, creating intimacy and validation.

3. **Contrast Structures**: The "This isn't X. It's Y." pattern used effectively:
   - "This isn't weakness. It's neurochemistry."
   - "This isn't a misunderstanding—it's an emotional boundary violation."
   - "This isn't love; it's acquisition."

4. **Decoder Card Structure**: Highly consistent format (Pattern → What They Say → What's Really Happening → How It Hooks You → Your Power Move).

5. **Diagnostic Questions**: Clear, actionable, memorable:
   - "After this interaction, do I feel more myself or less myself?"
   - "Is this impulse moving me toward my own healing and wellbeing, or away from it?"

### Voice Improvements Since Round 1

1. **First-Person Plural Reduction**: Fewer "we" intrusions. Most passages now maintain consistent "you" address.

2. **Stronger Verbs**: Active constructions have replaced many passive ones.

3. **Gender-Neutral Language**: Consistent throughout.

### Remaining Voice Issues

1. **Field Notes in Chapter 10**: Third-person literary fragments still break from established voice (see Margin Notes).

2. **Occasional "We" Slippage**: A few instances remain, primarily in family chapters. Not critical but could be polished.
