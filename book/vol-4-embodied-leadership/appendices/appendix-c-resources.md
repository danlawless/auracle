### What to Look For

When seeking support for embodied work:
- Trauma-informed approach
- Understanding of the nervous system
- Respect for your pace and boundaries
- No pressure to share before you're ready
- Their own grounded, regulated presence
- Experience with sexual healing (if relevant)
- Clear professional boundaries

### Red Flags

- Pushing you faster than your system is ready
- Using techniques without explaining them
- Touching without clear consent
- Blurring professional and personal boundaries
- Claiming one approach works for everyone
- Dismissing your experience or intuition

## Daily Practice Support

### Apps

**For Nervous System Regulation:**
- Insight Timer (free meditations)
- Calm or Headspace (guided practices)
- Othership (breathwork)

**For Tracking States:**
- Simple journal app

### Movement Practices

For embodiment and regulation:
- Yoga (especially trauma-sensitive)
- Dance (free movement, 5Rhythms)
- Tai Chi or Qigong
- Walking in nature

## Community and Connection

### Finding Your People

Look for:
- Groups where presence is valued over performance
- Communities that honor boundaries
- Spaces where you can be seen without being fixed
- People doing their own work

Avoid:
- Groups that require conformity for belonging
- Communities with unclear power dynamics
- Spaces where you consistently feel depleted
- Relationships that require self-abandonment

---

## Practice Integration

### Daily (5-15 minutes)

- Morning: Field scan + pre-day seal
- Evening: Post-day clearing

### Weekly (30-60 minutes)

- Extended practice (meditation, movement, nature)
- Digital audit
- Cord check

### Monthly (2+ hours)

- Full hygiene reset
- Relationship audit
- Longer retreat or solitude

---

## A Note on Resources

The goal isn't to find the perfect teacher, book, or practice.

The goal is to return to your body, trust your own signals, and lead from presence rather than performance.

Resources support the journey. They don't replace the work only you can do.

Take what serves. Leave what doesn't. Trust yourself to know the difference.
