## When Your Child Triggers You

Your child will activate old wounds. This is not failure—it's information.

| What's Happening | What It's Touching |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Their defiance | Your fear of powerlessness |
| Their neediness | Your exhaustion from being needed |
| Their anger | Your fear of anger in the home |
| Their similarity to your abuser | Unprocessed trauma |
| Their emotional intensity | Your own unmet emotional needs |

**The trigger is not about them. It's about what their behavior touches in you.**

---

## When Rage Rises

**In the moment:**

1. **Recognize** — "I am triggered right now"
2. **Pause** — Create physical space if possible
3. **Breathe** — Exhale longer than inhale (4-8)
4. **Buy time** — "I need a minute"
5. **Return** — When regulated, not before

**After the moment:**

1. Don't shame spiral
2. Repair when calm
3. Get curious about the trigger
4. Build capacity for next time

---

## The Repair Formula

When you mess up (and you will):

| Step | What To Say |
|------|-------------|
| **Name it** | "I yelled at you earlier, and that wasn't okay" |
| **Own it** | "That was my anger to manage, not yours to receive" |
| **Apologize genuinely** | "I'm sorry. You didn't deserve that" |
| **Explain without excusing** | "I was overwhelmed, but that's not your fault" |
| **Commit to growth** | "I'm working on handling my big feelings better" |
| **Reconnect** | Physical comfort, quality time, whatever fits |

**Repair matters more than perfection.**

## What Secure Attachment Looks Like

| Component | What It Looks Like in Action |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| **Safety** | Predictable responses, kept promises |
| **Attunement** | "I see you're feeling..." |
| **Soothing** | Physical comfort when distressed |
| **Support** | "You can do hard things. I'm here." |

## The Child Who Mirrors Your Wound

When one child triggers you more than others, ask:

- Do they remind me of someone?
- Do they act out what I couldn't express?
- Do they carry what I won't feel?
- Am I projecting my pain onto them?

**This child is not the problem. This child is the invitation.**

## Co-Parenting With a Narcissistic Ex

| Principle | Practice |
|-----------|----------|
| **Parallel parenting** | Minimize direct contact, coordinate logistics only |
| **Gray rock** | Boring, brief, emotionless responses |
| **Document everything** | Written records, screenshots, dates |
| **Don't badmouth** | Children need to form their own conclusions |
| **Focus on your home** | Create stability where you have control |
| **Therapy for children** | Give them safe space to process |

**You can't control their home. You can make yours a sanctuary.**

## When Grandparents Are the Problem

| Situation | Approach |
|-----------|----------|

## The Apology They Deserve

If you need to apologize for past behavior:

1. **Pick the right time** — When they're regulated and you're grounded
2. **Be specific** — Name what you did, not vague regret
3. **Don't make excuses** — Explain context without justifying harm
4. **Acknowledge impact** — "That must have felt..." / "I imagine that was..."
5. **Commit to change** — Describe what you're doing differently
6. **Allow their response** — They don't have to forgive immediately
