## Question Highlights

### Most Important Questions to Consider

**Q12 - Safety Warning Placement (CRITICAL)**
Safety warnings for potentially dangerous content (Exposure Questions, Exit Phase Escalation) appear mid-chapter or at the end. Activated readers in crisis may start reading before encountering warnings. Should these move to line 1?

**Q15 - Economic Privilege (CRITICAL)**
Author bio mentions expensive global healing journey without acknowledging privilege. Might create "healing = expensive" association despite excellent "Healing Without Financial Resources" section in Appendix A.

**Q9 - "No Contact" and Collectivist Cultures (HIGH)**
Book strongly advocates "no contact" as optimal healing path. For readers from collectivist cultures (many Asian, Latinx, Middle Eastern, African), family estrangement can mean loss of entire community, social death, economic devastation. Would "Boundaries While Maintaining Contact" section serve these readers?

**Q16 - Race and Narcissistic Abuse (HIGH)**
Race is never explicitly discussed. How do racism and narcissistic abuse intersect? Should the book address racialized gaslighting, weaponized stereotypes, police as unsafe resource for BIPOC survivors, or acknowledge what's outside scope?

**Q7 - "Mother/Father Wound" Structure (HIGH)**
Despite excellent note acknowledging non-traditional families, chapter structure still separates "Mother Wound" (nurturing failure) and "Father Wound" (achievement pressure). Does this reinforce gender stereotypes? Would "Primary/Secondary Caregiver Wound" framing work better?

## Final Reflection

This book will help survivors. The trauma portrayal is careful, the language is respectful, the tools are practical, and the core message—you can trust yourself—is vital.

These 18 questions are offered not as demands for changes, but as invitations to consider how the work might land with diverse communities across:
- Cultures (collectivist vs. individualist)
- Abilities (physical, cognitive, sensory)
- Identities (gender, sexuality, race)
- Circumstances (economic, legal, social)

The author's engagement with three rounds of sensitivity review demonstrates commitment to getting this right. That willingness to listen and learn is itself valuable and rare.
