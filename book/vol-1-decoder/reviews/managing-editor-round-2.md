## Part 1: Synthesized Feedback by Priority

### CRITICAL PRIORITY (Must Fix Before Publication)

These 6 issues appeared across multiple specialist reviews and represent fundamental quality concerns.

#### C1: Gender Pronouns in Chapter 3
**Sources:** Line Editor (Pattern Report), Sensitivity Reader
**Status:** Consensus — Must Fix

**Issue:** Chapter 3 contains 15+ instances of gender-specific pronouns (he/him/his) in pull quotes and examples, contradicting the front matter's gender-neutral statement and creating implicit bias.

**Examples:**
- Line 45: "He'll break your meditation to prove your focus belongs to him."
- Line 72: "He doesn't shout because he's angry—he shouts to rewrite the room's frequency."
- Line 443: "His 'I'm broken' isn't vulnerability—it's a strategy"

**Resolution:** Global search-and-replace to "they/them/their" OR reframe sentences to avoid pronouns.

**Action Required:** Yes — before publication

---

#### C2: Content Warnings Missing from Sensitive Chapters
**Sources:** Sensitivity Reader, Beta Reader
**Status:** Consensus — Must Fix

**Issue:** Several chapters contain graphic or activating content without content warnings:
- Chapter 3: Physical intimidation, suicide mentions (no warning)
- Chapter 17: Safety warning appears at END of chapter (should be at START)
- Chapter 18: Detailed sexual assault descriptions (no warning)

**Resolution:**
1. Add content warning at start of Chapter 3
2. Move Chapter 17 safety warning to beginning
3. Add explicit content warning before Chapter 18 sexuality section

**Action Required:** Yes — before publication

---

#### C3: Voice Break — Chapter 18 Field Notes
**Sources:** Line Editor (Prose Assessment, Pattern Report)
**Status:** Critical — Breaks Reader Trust

**Issue:** Lines 286-326 shift completely from established second-person voice to third-person literary fragments:
> "She is young. A boy wants something she doesn't. She feels the moment stretch..."

This breaks the reader trust built through 17 chapters of direct address.

**Resolution:** Convert to second person:
> "You're young. Someone wants something you don't want to give. You feel the moment stretch..."

**Action Required:** Yes — before publication

---

#### C4: Voice Break — Chapter 16 Sovereignty Statement
**Sources:** Line Editor (Prose Assessment, Margin Notes), Beta Reader
**Status:** Critical — May Feel Untrue to Crisis Readers

**Issue:** Lines 143-166 shift from recovery manual voice to affirmation genre:
> "I am surrounded by authentic people... I am loved by many for how amazing I am..."

For readers still in crisis, these statements may feel untrue or premature.

**Resolution:** Reframe as aspirational future rather than present-tense declarations. Acknowledge current reality while orienting toward healing.

**Action Required:** Yes — before publication

---

#### C5: Cross-Reference Inconsistencies
**Sources:** Developmental Editor (EDITORIAL-REVIEW.md), Proofreader

- Some chapters reference 10-chapter structure while manuscript has 18 chapters

**Status Update:** Proofreader fixed 4 cross-reference errors. Full audit recommended.

**Resolution:** Complete cross-reference audit using grep search before publication.

**Action Required:** Yes — verify all references

## Part 2: Conflict Resolution

### Resolved Conflicts

#### Conflict A: Chapter 17 Placement
**Positions:**
- Developmental Editor: Move to Part II (diagnostic tool positioning)
- Beta Reader: Keep accessible (powerful exposure questions)

**Resolution:** Keep current placement but add explicit triage guidance at chapter start:
> "Use this chapter if you're still questioning whether this is narcissism. If you've already identified the pattern, proceed to the Decoder Cards in Chapters 11-13."

**Rationale:** Preserves accessibility while clarifying purpose.

---

#### Conflict B: Redundancy Treatment
**Positions:**
- Some reviewers: Consolidate repeated techniques into single reference
- Others: Keep repetition for standalone chapter usability

**Resolution:**
1. Keep some strategic repetition for reference use
2. Create "Core Techniques Quick Reference" appendix for frequently-used tools
3. Add cross-references where techniques appear multiple times

**Rationale:** Balances comprehensiveness with efficiency.

---

#### Conflict C: Body Wisdom Claims
**Positions:**
- Line Editor: "Your body never lies" is too absolute
- Sensitivity Reader: May mislead readers with trauma-conditioned responses

**Resolution:** Revise to nuanced framing:
> "Your body tells its truth. Sometimes that's about now; sometimes it's about then."

**Rationale:** Validates somatic wisdom while acknowledging PTSD responses.

---

### Open Conflicts Requiring Author Decision

#### Decision Required: Chapter 18 Restructuring
**Options:**
- A: Move Chapter 18 to Part II after Chapter 8 (Developmental Editor recommendation)
- B: Keep current structure, add stronger transitions

**Impact:** Option A is significant restructuring affecting chapter numbering throughout.
**Recommendation:** Author decision needed on scope tolerance.

---

#### Decision Required: Chapter 5 Split
**Options:**
- A: Split into Types (Ch 5) and Roles (Ch 6), renumber subsequent chapters
- B: Keep combined, add internal navigation and breaks

**Impact:** Option A cascades renumbering through entire manuscript.
**Recommendation:** Depends on timeline and restructuring appetite.
