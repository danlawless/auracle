## Core References

The concepts in this book are grounded in established research across neuroscience, attachment theory, and somatic psychology.

---

### Polyvagal Theory

**Porges, S. W. (2011).** *The Polyvagal Theory: Neurophysiological Foundations of Emotions, Attachment, Communication, and Self-Regulation.* W.W. Norton & Company.

This work provides the foundation for understanding:
- How the nervous system determines safety vs. threat
- The role of the vagus nerve in social engagement
- Why "calm" can feel uncomfortable for trauma-adapted systems
- How co-regulation works at a physiological level

---

### Interpersonal Neurobiology

**Siegel, D. J. (2012).** *The Developing Mind: How Relationships and the Brain Interact to Shape Who We Are* (2nd ed.). Guilford Press.

Key concepts applied in this book:
- Integration as the goal of healthy development
- The relationship between attachment and brain structure
- The "window of tolerance" and regulation
- Mindsight and self-awareness

---

### Fear, Anxiety, and the Brain

**LeDoux, J. (2015).** *Anxious: Using the Brain to Understand and Treat Fear and Anxiety.* Viking.

This research informs:
- The distinction between fear and anxiety at a neural level
- Why insight alone doesn't change behavior
- The role of the amygdala in threat detection
- How habits form and can be reformed

---

### Stress, Behavior, and Biology

**Sapolsky, R. (2017).** *Behave: The Biology of Humans at Our Best and Worst.* Penguin Press.

Relevant to this book:
- How early environment shapes stress response
- The biology of self-control and decision-making
- Why context matters more than willpower
- The neuroscience of moral behavior and boundaries

---

### Emotion Construction

**Feldman Barrett, L. (2017).** *How Emotions Are Made: The Secret Life of the Brain.* Houghton Mifflin Harcourt.

This work supports:
- The constructed nature of emotional experience
- Why naming emotions changes them
- The role of interoception in emotional awareness
- How emotional concepts can be updated

---

### Social Neuroscience

**Lieberman, M. D. (2013).** *Social: Why Our Brains Are Wired to Connect.* Crown.

Key applications:
- Why social rejection activates pain circuits
- The neural basis of empathy and mentalizing
- How social connection regulates physiology
- The costs and benefits of social sensitivity

---

### Neuroplasticity and Positive Change

**Hanson, R. (2013).** *Hardwiring Happiness: The New Brain Science of Contentment, Calm, and Confidence.* Harmony.

Informs this book's approach to:
- How positive experiences can be encoded
- The negativity bias and how to work with it
- Practical methods for neural rewiring
- Why repetition matters more than intensity

---

### Trauma and the Body

**van der Kolk, B. (2014).** *The Body Keeps the Score: Brain, Mind, and Body in the Healing of Trauma.* Viking.

Foundation for:
- Somatic approaches to trauma healing
- Why the body stores traumatic memory
- The limits of talk therapy alone
- Integration of body-based practices
