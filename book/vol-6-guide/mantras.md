# Mantras for the Moment

## Grounding Phrases for Those Who Hold Others

*These are words you can return to when helping feels like losing yourself.*

---

### When You're Depleted

- I cannot pour from empty.
- My depletion is information, not failure.
- Rest is part of the practice, not a pause from it.
- I am allowed to need what I give.
- Sustainability is not weakness.

---

### When Helping Is Hiding

- My healing does not live inside their progress.
- I can be present without escaping into their story.
- Service is not a substitute for my own work.
- I notice when I'm avoiding myself through helping.
- Their needs do not erase mine.

---

### When You Take On Their Pain

- I can witness without absorbing.
- Their story can move through me without staying.
- I clear what isn't mine at the end of every session.
- I hold space—not weight.
- Compassion does not require co-suffering.

---

### When You Need to Be Needed

- My worth exists apart from being useful.
- I can be valuable without being indispensable.
- If they don't need me, that's success—not rejection.
- I release the need for their need.
- I am whole without being helpful.

---

### When You're Triggered by Their Material

- My activation is information about me, not about them.
- I can hold space while also needing space.
- I am allowed to be affected and remain present.
- Their story touched my story. That's human.
- I take my material to my own support, not to them.

---

### When It's Time to Refer

- Knowing my limits is integrity.
- Referring is not abandonment. It's care.
- I can't be all things to all people.
- Passing the baton is part of the work.
- Their healing doesn't depend on me alone.

---

### When Boundaries Feel Unkind

- Boundaries are not rejection. They're clarity.
- I can be boundaried and still be caring.
- My limits protect both of us.
- A container with no walls holds nothing.
- Saying no to more means saying yes to better.

---

### When You Forget Why You Started

- I don't have to save everyone to matter.
- One person held well is enough.
- My presence is the offering—not my performance.
- This work is sacred even when it's hard.
- I return to why I started, not what I've become.

---

### The Foundation Mantras

*Return to these when nothing else reaches you:*

- My healing is my credential. My boundaries are my offering.
- I cannot give what I don't have.
- I serve from overflow, not from depletion.
- Their growth does not validate my worth.
- I hold others' healing without losing my own.

---

*These mantras are guardrails. Return to them when the road gets narrow.*
